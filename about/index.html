About Mastery Quests

Practice should respond to the learner.

Mastery Quests is an adaptive educational gaming platform designed to turn retrieval practice, corrective feedback, and targeted review into a coherent path toward mastery.

Practice Repair Bridge Master

The purpose

Make practice more diagnostic, more responsive, and more useful.

Many review activities present the same sequence to every student and stop after marking an answer right or wrong. Mastery Quests uses performance, question history, learning-objective tags, response patterns, and recent difficulty to shape what the student encounters next.

The game layer provides momentum and identity. The learning layer determines the route.

Design commitments

Built around learning decisions, not decoration alone.

Adaptive retrieval

Questions are selected from structured pools using recent performance, concept history, fluency, and learning-objective coverage.

Targeted recovery

A missed concept can activate repair and bridge questions intended to address the underlying gap before the student moves on.

Transparent reporting

Completed runs summarize accuracy, time, streaks, strengths, weaknesses, and recommended priorities for additional review.

Faculty authorship

Templates and tools allow instructors to replace the disciplinary content and visual identity without rebuilding the adaptive engine from scratch.

Accessible choice

Multiple modes support different study goals while sound controls, keyboard access, readable interfaces, and reduced-motion preferences remain part of the design.

Evidence over novelty

The platform is intended to be evaluated through student performance, usability, instructional outcomes, and transparent design documentation.

Creator

Designed by an economics educator.

Mastery Quests was created by William Jennings Byrd, Ph.D., an Associate Professor and Associate Chair at Troy University. His teaching focuses on principles of economics, intermediate economics, and managerial economics, with research interests in economic education and economic history.

Mastery Quests is an independent educational project and is not an official Troy University website or product.
