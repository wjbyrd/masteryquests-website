Missions

Complete the realm

Students move from foundational market analysis to national performance, macroeconomic equilibrium, money, and stabilization policy.

Markets, scarcity, and elasticity

The Market Gate

Begin with the logic of choice and exchange. Students work through scarcity, opportunity cost, supply and demand, market intervention, surplus, elasticity, and the interpretation of shifting market conditions.

Launch Game

National accounting, labor, and growth

The National Ledger

Move from individual markets to the national economy. The mission develops GDP measurement, inflation, unemployment, productivity, living standards, and the foundations of long-run economic growth.

Launch Game

Aggregate demand and aggregate supply

The Equilibrium Crisis

Analyze short-run macroeconomic fluctuations through aggregate demand and aggregate supply. Students diagnose demand and supply shocks, inflationary and recessionary gaps, and the economy's adjustment toward equilibrium.

Launch Game

Money, banking, and monetary transmission

The Liquidity Grid

Explore the financial system through money creation, bank balance sheets, reserve behavior, the quantity theory of money, liquidity preference, interest rates, and the transmission of monetary policy.

Launch Game

Monetary and fiscal stabilization

The Stabilization Protocol

Conclude the realm by applying monetary and fiscal policy to inflation, unemployment, recession, crowding out, policy lags, and the tradeoffs involved in stabilizing the macroeconomy.
